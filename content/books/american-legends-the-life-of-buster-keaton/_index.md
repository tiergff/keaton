## 简介（机翻）

　　“据说卓别林希望你喜欢他，但基顿不在乎。我觉得他在乎，但他太骄傲了，不敢问。他的电影避免了卓别林电影中的悲情和情感，通常刻画的是是一个活泼的年轻人看到一个目标，并在最令人畏惧的障碍面前努力实现。巴斯特在龙卷风、瀑布、巨石雪崩和从高处坠落中幸存下来，从不停下来鞠躬：他的眼睛盯着自己的目标。他的电影，从整体上看，就像面对逆境时持续的乐观行为；令人惊讶的是，他如何不经询问就赢得了我们的钦佩和温柔。——罗杰·艾伯特

　　历史上最有影响力的人物的生活被写过很多了，但是有多少森林为树木而消失？在查尔斯河编辑的《美国传奇》系列书中，读者可以快速了解美国最重要的人物的生活，只需通勤的时间，同时还可以了解一些早已被遗忘或从未为人所知的趣事。

　　20世纪20年代，蓬勃发展的电影产业开始崭露头角，与演员兼导演查理·卓别林一起，巴斯特·基顿处于好莱坞的巅峰。毫无疑问，基顿在无声电影中表现如此出色，因为他一直在家庭杂耍表演中练习喜剧，扮演“不会受伤的小男孩”，在5岁时就成为一名受欢迎的演员。事实上，他的身体喜剧形式，最初是让他的父亲把他扔在舞台上，在银幕上得到了很好的诠释，而他的一些闹剧和其他喜剧噱头甚至在今天仍然具有传奇色彩，部分原因是基顿自己表演了特技-。事实上，基顿自己写素材，对《马克思兄弟》（The Marx Brothers）和《活宝三人组》（The Three Stooges）等戏剧产生了重要的喜剧影响。

　　基顿除了是他那个时代最重要的喜剧演员之一、自己写东西外，他还导演了许多自己的电影，在电影还相对较新的时候，他掌握了摄像机的使用。正如《时代》杂志作家理查德·科利斯所指出的，“看着他那美丽而紧凑的身体在扭曲的排列中旋转或扭成椒盐卷饼，甚至在周围的一切都疯狂的时候，他静静地优雅地站着。看着他的心灵在注视着一个充满敌意的宇宙，巴斯特理解、承受着这个宇宙的狂暴，而且奇迹般地驯服了它。看着他的相机为他拍摄（基顿导演或监督了他所有最好的电影）；他像玻璃中捕捉到的恒星一样酷……这种媒介仍处于起步阶段；漫画开创了让人们对移动图像发笑的技术。事实证明，基顿对这一切都了如指掌——凭直觉。”

　　当美国电影协会（American Film Institute）评选出20世纪最伟大的演员时，基顿排在第21位，在无声电影明星中仅次于卓别林，但他向有声电影的过渡并不顺利。基顿已经形成了一种面无表情的凝视，无论他的角色发生了什么，或什么特技或“事故”降临在他身上，这在杂耍和无声电影中增加了喜剧效果，但当声音将焦点转移到对话时，基顿的身体喜剧就不受欢迎了。尽管如此，他在今天仍然非常受欢迎和有影响力，当代明星如金·凯瑞、凯文·史派西和成龙都在纪念作品中表达了对他的钦佩。

　　《美国传奇：巴斯特·基顿的一生》探讨了美国最著名的喜剧演员之一的生活和职业生涯。伴随着重要人物、地点和事件的照片，您将以前所未有的方式了解。

&#8220;It&#8217;s said that Chaplin wanted you to like him, but Keaton didn&#8217;t care. I think he cared, but was too proud to ask. His films avoid the pathos and sentiment of the Chaplin pictures, and usually feature a jaunty young man who sees an objective and goes for it in the face of the most daunting obstacles. Buster survives tornados, waterfalls, avalanches of boulders, and falls from great heights, and never pauses to take a bow: He has his eye on his goal. And his movies, seen as a group, are like a sustained act of optimism in the face of adversity; surprising, how without asking, he earns our admiration and tenderness.” – Roger Ebert

A lot of ink has been spilled covering the lives of history’s most influential figures, but how much of the forest is lost for the trees? In Charles River Editors’ American Legends series, readers can get caught up to speed on the lives of America’s most important men and women in the time it takes to finish a commute, while learning interesting facts long forgotten or never known.

In the 1920s, the burgeoning movie industry was starting to come into its own, and alongside actor and director Charlie Chaplin, Buster Keaton was at the peak of Hollywood. It’s no surprise that Keaton was so effective in silent films, because he had been practicing comedy in his family’s vaudeville acts as “The Little Boy Who Can’t Be Damaged”, becoming a popular performer by the age of 5. Indeed, his physical form of comedy, which initially involved having his father throwing him around the stage, translated well onto the screen, and some of his slapstick and other comic gags remain legendary even today, in part because Keaton practiced his own stunts. In fact, Keaton wrote his own material and was a crucial comic influence on acts like The Marx Brothers and The Three Stooges.

In addition to being one of the foremost comedians of his time and writing his own stuff, Keaton also directed many of his own films, and he mastered the use of cameras at a time when film was still relatively new. As Time magazine writer Richard Corliss noted, “Watch his beautiful, compact body as it pirouettes or pretzels in tortured permutations or, even more elegantly, stands in repose as everything goes crazy around it. Watch his mind as it contemplates a hostile universe whose violent whims Buster understands, withstands and, miraculously, tames. Watch his camera taking his picture (Keaton directed or supervised all his best films); it is as cool as the star it captured in its glass&#8230; The medium was still in its infancy; comics were pioneering the craft of making people laugh at moving images. Keaton, it turns out, knew it all — intuitively.”

When the American Film Institute listed the greatest actors of the 20th century, Keaton came in 21st, and second among the silent film stars behind Chaplin, but the transition to making talkies didn’t go as smoothly for him. Keaton had developed a deadpan stare no matter what happened to his character or what stunt or “accident” befell him, which added comedic effect in vaudeville and silent films, but when sound moved the focus to dialogue, Keaton’s physical brand of comedy didn’t go over as well. Nonetheless, he remains incredibly popular and influential today, with contemporary stars like Jim Carrey, Kevin Spacey, and Jackie Chan all expressing their admiration for him in commemorative pieces.

American Legends: The Life of Buster Keaton examines the life and career of one of America’s most famous comic actors. Along with pictures of important people, places, and events, you will learn about Jean Arthur like never before, in no time at all.
